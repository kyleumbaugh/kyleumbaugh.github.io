The Purdue Arboretum

The Purdue Arboretum encompasses the entire campus of Purdue University — a living collection of thousands of trees, shrubs, and plants spread across West Lafayette, Indiana. The Horticulture Park serves as a curated centerpiece, with labeled specimens perfect for guided learning.

Tree Spotlight

Three remarkable trees found along the Purdue Arboretum's Spring Interest Tour — each with a story worth knowing.

#12
🌿
Common Pawpaw
Asimina triloba
A native understory tree with large, tropical-looking leaves that produces a custard-flavored fruit — the largest edible fruit native to North America.
Fun Fact: Pawpaws were a favorite of George Washington and Thomas Jefferson, both of whom grew them at their estates. The fruit tastes like a cross between banana and mango.
🍂 Best in late summer–fall
#15
🌸
Tulip Tree
Liriodendron tulipifera
A tall native tree with uniquely shaped leaves and tulip-shaped greenish-yellow flowers. One of the tallest eastern hardwoods — can reach over 150 feet.
Fun Fact: The Tulip Tree is Indiana's state tree. Native Americans hollowed single trunks to make dugout canoes large enough to carry 20 people.
🌸 Blooms May–June
#4
🌰
Ohio Buckeye
Aesculus glabra
A native tree with distinctive palmate leaves (5 leaflets) and yellow-green flower clusters. Produces the shiny brown nuts known as "buckeyes."
Fun Fact: The dark mark on the nut resembles a deer's eye — hence "buckeye." Carrying one is a traditional good luck charm, which is likely how Ohio State University got its mascot.
🌼 Flowers in April–May

Visit the Arboretum

The Purdue Arboretum is free, open year-round, and accessible from anywhere on campus.

📍
Location
Purdue University Campus, West Lafayette, IN 47907. The Horticulture Park is located near the Horticulture Building on S. University Street.
🕐
Hours
The arboretum grounds are accessible year-round during daylight hours. No admission fee or reservation required for self-guided visits.
🚗
Parking
Visitor parking is available in the Horticulture Lot off Marsteller Street. Metered parking is also available along S. University Street.
♿
Accessibility
Paved paths connect many arboretum locations. The Horticulture Park has accessible walkways throughout the main garden areas.
📬
Group Visits & Field Trips
For organized groups, contact the Purdue HLA department to coordinate your visit.
